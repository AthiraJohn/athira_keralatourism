GOD'S OWN COUNTRY

Kerala has been a popular place for travellers since a very long time. The wealth of natural beauty in the form of placid backwaters, lush greenery, picturesque hill towns, and beautiful beaches has resulted in the name God's Own Country.

There is a belief that, once a year during the Onam festival, Mahabali returns to Kerala. It is said that the god Vishnu is guarding Mahabali's Kingdom as a mark of respect for his virtues. These Puranic accounts portray Kerala as "God's Own Country", or the land favoured by God.

Kerala a place of refuge for Malayalees. This state is blessed with natural wealth like mountains, forests, beaches, wildlife etc. make the state perfect from the point of view of all tourism. Kerala is located at the southwest end of the Indian subcontinent. It is situated between the Upper Western Ghats in the east and the Arabian Sea in the west. Kerala also counted in the best places to visit in India.
